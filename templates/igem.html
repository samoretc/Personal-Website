iGEM Website

During the summer of 2013, I was a part of the Rose-Hulman International Genetic Engineered Machine (iGEM) team. In brief, our project aimed to transform Yeast and E. Coli, so they would need each other to survive. As a part of the competition, we needed to build a website. Click here to reach it.

As a part of our project, I made the canvas simulation shown below. Our model consisted of a two by two set of differential equations. The simulation shows the phase plan as well as the solution to this system of differential equations. You can drag and drop the blue dot around (the initial concentrations) to determine if we predict the yeast and bacteria population to survive or die.
